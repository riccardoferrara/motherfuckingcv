Presentation

I oversaw the implementation of this platform, which facilitated customer support ticketing, R&D ticketing, non-conformity management, product releases, versioning, pay-per-use usage tokens, licensing, invoicing, internal and external certification training, and clinical validation. During my time at Sim&Cure, I also played a vital role in scaling the company during two fundraising rounds and launching our satellite office in the United States, where I worked closely with customers to evangelize our technology.

Following my tenure at Sim&Cure, I designed and developed several exciting projects for the Italian railway network, including interactive maps (using Node, Python, and HTML), a purchasing request management platform (utilizing SQL), and weather alert systems (using Node and Python). Additionally, I created an e-commerce platform for another client using WordPress, JavaScript, and HTML. Recently, I had the pleasure of teaching at Tortuga Coders, a highly regarded web development bootcamp, where I taught courses on Node, SQL, React, and Express.

Currently, I hold the position of Lead Developer at Foundever (formerly known as Sitel Group), one of the world's largest outsourcing companies. In this role, I continue to utilize my extensive skills in software design, development, and implementation, while also taking on new challenges and opportunities for growth.

Postdoctoral Researcher at Irras Technology and University of Montpellier | 04/2015 - 10/2015 | Montpellier

As a postdoctoral researcher, I conducted a study aimed at optimizing intracranial medical devices (IMDs) for the treatment of intracranial aneurysms. Specifically, I evaluated the influence of geometrical parameters of arteries and flow-diverter devices on bloodstream and aneurysm thrombosis. To achieve this, I utilized different mock-up/models to evaluate the pressure drop due to the presence of flow-diverter devices. Additionally, I developed a numerical model that accurately predicts the pressure drop and thrombosis phenomenon in in-vivo cases. This model was calibrated and tested by comparing it to experimental results. The results of this study have the potential to significantly improve the efficacy and safety of IMDs for the treatment of intracranial aneurysms.

Inventor | 10/2014 - 04/2015 | Montpellier

Driven by my passion for biomechanics and inspired by my mentor, who directed my recently completed PhD thesis, I became deeply interested in endovascular treatment using flow-diverters for cerebral aneurysms. In the following months, I independently and voluntarily developed an invention that marked the beginning of a thrilling journey: a medical device that can simulate the behavior of prostheses in real-time, based on the patient's scanned images (Cone Beam CT). This invention provides neuroradiologists with the ability to choose the most suitable prosthesis and receive guidance during the procedure. Today I am the named inventor of this patented invention and I am very proud of it.

For the geeks out there: I developed the entire prototype using Python, leveraging the VTK, ITK, and CTK libraries. To bring the prototype to life, I had to blend my computer science skills with my engineering knowledge, starting from a 3D vectorization of arteries from grayscale images, and then creating a mechanical model of prostheses that could be calculated quickly yet accurately (similar to FEM).
